Nadia is kneeling next to the tub in tears. "What happened while I was away?"

I look at her blood-drained face and realize what this must have looked like to her. I quickly climb out of the bathtub. I sway, but Nadia is there to steady me. Even though I'm soaked, she throws her arms around me, hugging me tight. Suddenly overwhelmed with emotion, I bury my head in her shoulder. I'm lucky to still be alive.

Nadia seems to realize that I'm crying now too, because she slowly pulls away to look at me. I just slump down to the floor, covering my face with my water-wrinkled hands. "I'm sorry," I say. My shoulders are shaking. I can't control it.

Nadia takes a seat by my side, accompanying me quietly as my tears flow down the sides of my face.

✦ ✦ ✦

It takes me a while to finally calm down. Nadia picks out a clean outfit for me to change into, and then the two of us are sitting side by side in the bedroom. Nadia's taken my laptop and put on a show. As we sit there on the bed, I feel Nadia's fingers lightly brush mine, and I flip my hand over. Her fingers twine through mine, and I hold her hand. Even though I've calmed down, I still feel awful. I scared Nadia pretty badly today. I also scared myself pretty badly.

My mind is a mess, and even as the show plays on the screen in front of us, it's difficult to pay attention. Nadia rests her head on my shoulder. Sitting there together, I find myself thinking that maybe once I can leave the apartment again, maybe we should take some time away from each other. It's hard for me to think about what my life would even look like without Nadia, but I don't want to scare her—or myself—like this again.

✦ ✦ ✦

Chapter Forty
Nadia

I spend a lot of the morning drafting a text to Melissa. I wait an entire day after I send it, but there's no response. I try calling next, only for the phone to go straight to voicemail. I email her as well, but get the same result. When I still have yet to receive any sort of reply from her, I decide that instead of waiting around, I'll try to go get answers from Ethan instead.

I've seen Matteo's schedule, and by lingering around outside his class, I quickly identify Ethan from Matteo's description. Except—I don't know what to do but watch silently as he disappears down the hall.

✦ ✦ ✦

That afternoon, I'm at the modeling agency. When they have me step onto the scale today, I hold my breath as if that too can reduce my weight. I look at the number glowing back at me. I just made it. I got the job. I want to laugh—to smile—but I'm too tired now to do so.

"Nadia?" a voice calls my name. "The hairstylist is ready for you."

I blink. "Hairstylist?"

✦ ✦ ✦

Chapter Forty-One
Matteo

I hear the door open and close softly when Nadia returns. There's quiet for a long moment before she lets out a sob. It's a pained, heartbroken sound, and I jump out of my seat, abandoning the video game I had been in the middle of to find her slumped at the foot of the door. I stare. "Nadia, your hair!"

"M-my hair," Nadia manages to get out between sobs. She buries her face in her hands in despair. Her once long hair now barely reaches her shoulders. "I didn't know they were going to cut it off."

I kneel down beside her, but I hesitate. I want to reach out, but something holds me back. Instead, Nadia reaches out, shaking as she wraps her arms around me. I hold her quietly until she finally stops crying.

✦ ✦ ✦

That night, as we lie next to each other in bed, Nadia tells me everything. "I think I'm ill," she concludes, fiddling with the hem of her pajama shirt. "My physical and emotional state has been all over the place. Modeling is my dream, but after today, I've realized I don't want to die for it."

"Good," I say simply. "So you won't go back, then?"

Nadia nods determinedly. "I won't go back."

✦ ✦ ✦

Chapter Forty-Two
Nadia

Even though it's only been a few days, somehow the whole school seems to have found out about the incident with Melissa, and now everyone is gossiping about it too. On the school forums, conspiracies are being spread like wildfire, each crazier than the next. Someone had found Matteo's student ID photo, and now that's being circulated as well.

What doesn't help is the fact that Matteo's photo is poorly lit and not flattering in the slightest. It must have been from his freshman year too, because even I barely recognize him from it. Ethan's picture, on the other hand, is flawless. Ethan's even gained a following on the forums of female students, arguing that he's too good-looking to be guilty. Their main argument is that Matteo looks guilty and Ethan does not, which infuriates me every time I see any of their unsolicited comments.

I press my hands to my head. For the past week, my stomach has been empty, and that ever-present feeling of lightheadedness is back. Even though I've made the decision to not go back to the modeling agency, it's still hard for me to just . . . eat. I haven't told Matteo about it. He doesn't need another thing to worry about right now.

Just as I'm debating whether it would be in my favor to get something small from one of the cafés on campus, I get a notification on my phone. My heart rate quickens. Melissa has finally replied to me.

Are you on campus? Her short message reads. We can talk.

I hurry back to the student dorms, taking the elevator and walking down the hall to the room number Melissa sent in a follow-up message. When I reach the door, I find myself holding my breath as I press the doorbell. The door opens a few moments later. Melissa stands in the doorway. She looks a lot different from how I remember her; her face is bare of her usual makeup, and her hair is limp and uncurled.

"Come in," she says quietly.

I silently follow Melissa inside, and she shuts the door behind me. We're standing in the living room in silence for a long moment before I finally speak up. "Do you think you could tell me what happened that day? Please, Melissa. I want to know what happened."

"I don't know for sure what happened that day myself," Melissa says shortly. She walks over to the kitchen to start the hot water kettle. "All I know is that my friends and I were at the party. I had a little too much to drink. I remember being in someone's bedroom. I just wanted to go home. Matteo was there."

I open my mouth, but Melissa cuts me off.

"Before you say anything, I don't know and can't say whether Matteo did or did not do anything to me." Melissa takes a breath as she turns to look at me. "But I do know that he didn't do anything worse. If he had the intent, I'm lucky the police showed up when they did."

I'm quiet for a moment. "Do you remember anything else?"

"I don't know," Melissa says shortly. "The whole situation is just too confusing. I'd like to know what happened too."

I wrack my brains as Melissa pours me a cup of hot water. Since Melissa doesn't know, it seems like I have no choice but to go to Ethan next. I don't want to put myself in danger seeing him alone, and I find myself wondering if there is anyone else I know who can go with me. It would be for the best too if that person was male. Suddenly, someone pops into my mind, and I pull out my phone. I just don't know if he'll be willing . . .

✦ ✦ ✦

The next day, I'm waiting outside the dining hall when I spot the familiar figure. I wave to him, and he waves back as he comes to a stop in front of me.

"Thank you so much for showing up," I say breathlessly.

Bryan nods. "I don't mind helping you out pretending to be a buyer, but if it turns out Matteo is in the wrong after all, and Ethan doesn't sell recordings, I can't help you."

I let out a heavy sigh. "I know."

Bryan and I walk together to one of the academic buildings and linger in the hall outside the lecture hall. When the class ends, I retreat further into the hall while Bryan waits by the door. Ethan steps out shortly, and Bryan steps into his path.

In the long email I had sent Bryan the previous day, I had explained the best I could about Ethan selling shady recordings. If we could get our hands on physical evidence, at least it would show he wasn't innocent either.

Eventually, Ethan walks away, and Bryan returns to me. He just shakes his head. My heart sinks.

I drop my gaze. "Thank you for coming with me anyway."

"What are you planning to do next?"

"I don't know," I admit. "I'm under a lot of stress right now. I'm currently the only one working, and rent is due at the end of the month. If Matteo does get charged, there will undoubtedly be fines to pay as well."

Bryan's gaze softens. "I really hope you and Matteo can pull through."

"Thank you," I say quietly.

✦ ✦ ✦

That night, after Matteo has gone to bed, I sit alone in the living room. I want to cry. I'm at a loss when my phone lets off a soft buzz. Tiredly, I glance over at it. It's from Bryan.

My eyes grow wide as I read through his message. There's a file at the bottom. He managed to get it from Ethan after all.

✦ ✦ ✦

Chapter Forty-Three
Matteo

Even with Bryan exposing Ethan's illegal sales to the police, there's nothing it can do to prove I'm innocent. In the end, Ethan and I both end up with "improper conduct" on our records. It isn't a good feeling knowing that the label is going to follow me forever. When people ask, I don't know what I can tell them either.

Even though it's only been a week since the incident, in a way, it feels like years have passed. Nadia tried to comfort me too after the verdict came out, I can't help but feel disappointed in myself. After paying the fine, we're both feeling the strain financially.

I can tell that Nadia is really stretching herself thin with school and work, and I can tell that it's starting to take a toll on her, too. I don't want to be her burden.

It's the weekend after my temporary house arrest has been lifted, and I meander the streets until I eventually end up at the lake. I walk across the silent bridge. The fish all swim up to me again despite the fact that I don't have any food to give them this time.

I pull out my phone instead and send a message to Nadia. When are you out of class?

Her response comes quickly. Ten minutes left.

Feel up to meeting me at the lake? I have something important I want to discuss with you.

Okay.

I put my phone away and go back to watching the fish. Half an hour goes by, then an hour, then two. Wondering if something is holding Nadia up, I send another message to her. She doesn't respond. Maybe her phone is dead?

As I continue to wait, the answer comes to me on its own. The fish have long stopped swimming toward me, and the lake is eerily still. Maybe it's for the best that Nadia and I break up—or at least take a long break from each other. I think it might really be for the best.

✦ ✦ ✦

Chapter Forty-Four
Nadia

I don't know when or how I fainted, but by the time I wake up in the nurse's office, my heart sinks when I look at the time. There's no one else around, so I grab my bag and hurry as fast as I can across campus. The sun is setting, and I know I've missed work today. My phone is dead, but I still rush to the lake anyway.

When I arrive, besides a family with a young kid, I'm the only one around. I walk in silence along the walkway. The ducks are still drifting peacefully on the water, but Matteo is long gone.

✦ ✦ ✦

On the bus ride back, I sit alone, staring absently out the window. I managed to charge my phone at a nearby café, but as I sat there looking at Matteo's contact, I found I didn't know what to write. The sun is going down now, and I finally take out my phone to send him a message. Have you had dinner yet? If not, would you like to meet at the Japanese restaurant?

Matteo's response is short. Okay.

There's a chill in the air when I get off the bus. I stand there for a moment, watching as it drives away into the foggy night.

Matteo's already waiting for me at a table inside, and I silently slide into the chair across from him.

"Are you alright?" Matteo asks softly.

I nod. "I'm sorry I missed the meeting at the lake. I just wasn't feeling well today."

The two of us look over the menu. We order, and then continue to sit in silence until the food arrives, but neither of us touches our meals.

Matteo finally takes a breath. "I was thinking maybe we should take a break."

I silently process his words for a moment. "Why?"

"I'm afraid of becoming a burden to you."

"I'm afraid too." It's my turn to take a breath. "But I don't need us to always be safe. I just need us to be honest and still here."

Matteo is quiet now as he contemplates my response. Then some of the light seems to return to his eyes as he smiles faintly. "Okay. Then I'll keep trying, too."

✦ ✦ ✦

Chapter Forty-Five
Matteo

Even with the label following me around, I've decided to continue with therapy. Some of the people who were there when I started are gone now, and there are new faces in the room today. As I sit there, I find myself silently wondering whether they got better or if they had given up on themselves.

The campus rumors about me haven't completely subsided yet, but one day, while scrolling through one of those forums, I quietly closed it and set my phone aside. Those comments don't define me. And since then, I haven't gone back.

Nadia is back to modeling clothing, but this time, she switched over to an agency that would accept her the way she is. When I get home in the evenings, sometimes she'll already be at the dining room table, eating by herself. I've noticed that the majority of her food is still small and light, but any progress is good progress. She just finished her first shoot at her new agency today, and now we're going out to celebrate.

Nadia suggested Mexican food, so I found myself recommending the restaurant not far from campus. It feels like forever since I've been here as we step inside. It's familiar, but at the same time, everything feels different.

"Hello, dears," the waitress says as she comes over. She offers both of us a smile before addressing me. "I haven't seen you in a while. Welcome back. Is this your girlfriend? Such a pretty girl."

Nadia and I share a small smile.

We end up at a table near the back, and when our food arrives, I watch Nadia take happy bites of her burrito. "I told my parents about you," she says between bites. "For the first time in a while, I've felt brave enough to reach out to them."

"What did you tell them?"

"I told them I have a boyfriend now. I told them he's my best friend, that he's kind, and that he's trying his best."

I smile at that, feeling warm inside. "Come to think of it, I've been wanting to fix my relationship with my own parents."

"Maybe your therapist could offer some advice?" Nadia suggests.

"That's a good idea, actually. I'll bring it up with him at my next session." I pause. "I've also been wanting to go to the aquarium again. Would you like to go with me?"

Nadia smiles. "It's a date."

The chatter in the restaurant is light, and Nadia's fingers brush against mine beneath the table as we wait for the check. I hold her hand. Things almost feel normal.

Normal is something the two of us still have a ways to go to achieve, but we'll keep trying. Together.
