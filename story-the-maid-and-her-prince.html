The young prince sat in his room. He stood up, paced around, sat back down. “Come here,” the prince said eagerly.

The girl remained by the door, and the prince could feel her eyes watching him as he marched over to the closet and dug through it until he found a blanket. Once the prince had moved away from the alcove, the girl cautiously made her way over to it and took a seat. She drew her knees to her chest, her eyes never leaving the prince for an instant.

Trying his best to ignore her persistent stare, the prince sat down at his desk and pulled the stack of books toward him. His mood spoiled, he might as well do some homework. He couldn’t have spent more than five minutes reading before he opened one of the drawers and took out the biscuits he had smuggled from dinner. He lifted one to his mouth, but then he paused. The girl was still watching him, and he set the biscuit back down. “Are you hungry? You can have them.”

“Really?” the girl asked hopefully.

“I’m fat already,” the prince said in annoyance. “I don’t need them.”

The girl hesitantly slid down from the alcove and scurried over to snatch them off the table. Returning to the alcove just as quickly, she continued to watch the prince as she nibbled away at the biscuits.

After a while, the prince looked up from his books again. “Hey, what’s your name?”

“I know you like to laugh whenever you talk about him, but he’s not all that bad,” Ivanna pointed out. “He might have been a little immature, but he was also kind.”

Roland lifted the plate and held it out to her.

Roland shook his head. “All’s been quiet.”

Ivanna dipped her head, not sure whether this was good or bad news. Even after Roland left for his daily lessons, Ivanna lingered in his chambers. Roland’s face relaxed into a smile as he took one of Ivanna’s hands in his own. Ivanna blinked as he pressed something rounded into the palm of her hand. Ivanna examined the simple metal band. “A ring?”

Roland nodded. “I spent a while saving up for this one. I thought it was fitting. I left the old rings behind, so I wanted to give you one that actually meant something. I’ve been carrying it with me for a while now, trying to muster the courage to ask you if you would be my wife for real.”

Ivanna slipped the ring onto her finger before wrapping her arms around Roland. “I love it,” she breathed.

Outside, the morning fog was beginning to lift from the fields, and Ivanna knew that the life they had built together was finally their own.
