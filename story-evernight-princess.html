The Moon, The Stars, And The Evernight Princess

By Sienna Sunflower

Lauren Locke sat by the window, looking out at the town lights that glittered like miniature stars in the distance. She absently combed her fingers through her long black hair. From the castle, she was the closest to the stars she'd get, but tonight, a layer of clouds had blanketed the night sky.

Princess Lauren of Velmoor — the kingdom once known across the world as the "Evernight Kingdom" under her late, tyrannical father — lives a quiet life at her twin brother King Evander's side, helping him rebuild the country's reputation after their father's Thirty Years of Terror. Evander, distant and seemingly emotionless since childhood, wears a silver mask and rarely lets anyone close, haunted by the accidental death of their other brother years earlier and the guilt of a throne he never wanted. Lauren, meanwhile, carries a secret of her own: years ago, she discovered an ancient book of magic hidden beneath the castle, and ever since, dark markings have crept across her hands, granting her powers she barely understands.

✦ ✦ ✦

When Eloria's newly elected princess, Emmeline Estone — a commoner chosen by lottery rather than birth — arrives at Velmoor Castle, a warm friendship blossoms between the two princesses. Together with her maid Arabella and Emmeline's knight escorts Aldric and Octavian, Lauren discovers that her brother has long been secretly exchanging love letters with a nobleman named Alexis Fairchild, the only person who has ever truly reached the guarded heart beneath Evander's mask. With Lauren and Emmeline's help, the two finally meet in person and share their feelings for the first time.

But when Lauren's magic is revealed publicly after a heroic river rescue, fear spreads through both kingdoms, and an assassin is sent after her. To protect her, Evander sends Lauren away to live quietly at Emmeline's grandmother's countryside home, where — away from the castle's expectations — Lauren finally learns to master her magic, mend what was broken, and understand who she truly is.

When she eventually returns to Velmoor Castle to formally address both kingdoms' fears, she does so not by hiding her power, but by showing them exactly who she is: a princess who only ever wanted peace for the people and family she loves. Back at the castle, as letters between Evander and Alexis continue to fly by messenger hawk, Lauren receives happy news of her own from Emmeline — an invitation to her wedding to Octavian — a fitting close to a story about finding one's true family, wherever it may be found.

✦ End ✦
