The Maid And Her Prince


The young prince sat in his room. He stood up, paced around, sat back down. It was night, and the glass of the window was a mirror, reflecting his room and the flickering light of the candle-lit lamps back at him. He was thirteen, restless, and had just started his adolescence. As such, his desire to sleep with a girl was the only thing on his mind at night. He was feeling it especially tonight and jumped to his feet again. He wobbled over to the door as fast as his chubby legs could carry him and threw it open.
        “Mr. Becker!” the prince called into the stone hallway, his voice echoing.
        It didn’t take long before the middle-aged man came running. “You called, Your Highness?”
        “Get me one of the maids.”
        “Er, is there a particular maid you have in mind, Your Highness?”
        “One that’s around my age. And pretty.”
        “Yes, Your Highness.”
        The prince returned to his room, pacing a few more times before sitting down on his bed, winded. Shortly, there was a knock on the door. It opened, and a girl stepped into the room. She looked petrified as the door swung closed behind her.
        “Come here,” the prince said eagerly.
        The girl didn’t move. Instead, she burst into tears.
        The prince stared at her in surprise, completely taken aback. “D-don’t cry. What’s wrong?” She looked so small standing there that he suddenly felt embarrassed.
        The girl just kept sobbing. “I don’t want to sleep with you. I’m young and beautiful, and you’re fat and ugly.”
        The prince opened his mouth, but he closed it again when no words came out. “Well, I don’t want to sleep with you anymore,” he shot back sourly. “You’re not even that pretty. You can go.”
        “Mr. Becker said he’d collect me in the morning,” the maid sniffled as she wiped at her tear-streaked face. I can’t leave on my own—it’s past curfew.”
        “Fine,” the prince said crossly. “Stay here, then. But you sleep over there.” He pointed to the alcove in front of the window. “I’ll find a blanket for you.”
        The girl remained by the door, and the prince could feel her eyes watching him as he marched over to the closet and dug through it until he found a blanket. Once the prince had moved away from the alcove, the girl cautiously made her way over to it and took a seat. She drew her knees to her chest, her eyes never leaving the prince for an instant.
        Trying his best to ignore her persistent stare, the prince sat down at his desk and pulled the stack of books toward him. His mood spoiled, he might as well do some homework. He couldn’t have spent more than five minutes reading before he opened one of the drawers and took out the biscuits he had smuggled from dinner. He lifted one to his mouth, but then he paused. The girl was still watching him, and he set the biscuit back down. “Are you hungry? You can have them.”
        “Really?” the girl asked hopefully.
        “I’m fat already,” the prince said in annoyance. “I don’t need them.”
        The girl hesitantly slid down from the alcove and scurried over to snatch them off the table. Returning to the alcove just as quickly, she continued to watch the prince as she nibbled away at the biscuits.
        After a while, the prince looked up from his books again. “Hey, what’s your name?”
        The girl blinked before slowly sitting up a little straighter. “Ivanna.”


Ten Years Later

Her long brown hair spilled over the wood, and she brushed it out of her face as she sat up. After sitting in silence for a moment, she glanced over at the handsome young man behind the desk across the room. His curly red hair seemed to glow in the light, and he smiled. “Good morning.”

        The prince smiled faintly. “What is there to clean? We already dusted everything yesterday, remember? I’ll clean up again after tonight. Don’t worry about it.”
        The maid nodded. She gazed out the window as she tied her hair up. Then she turned to look at the prince again. “Roland . . .”
        Roland looked up. “Ivanna?”
        Ivanna sighed softly as she looked away. “I dreamed about our first meeting last night.”
        Roland let out a laugh. “I’ll apologize to you for seeing that old me, then.”
        “I know you like to laugh whenever you talk about him, but he’s not all that bad,” Ivanna pointed out. “He might have been a little immature, but he was also kind.”
        “And chubby,” Roland added.
        Ivanna gave him an exasperated smile at that. “If you don’t need any help here, I’ll head back down to the maid’s quarters. We’ll be setting up for the ball, after all. I’ll be back tomorrow morning.”
