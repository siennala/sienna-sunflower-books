The Golden Sunlight In The Summer Is The Light That Paints The Castle In Color

By Sienna Sunflower

It was a clear summer day, and a soft breeze wafted in through the open window behind the young king's desk. The king had fallen asleep with his head on the table, and the documents he'd been poring over were pushed off to the side along with his golden crown. Smiling faintly, the knight standing next to him carefully placed his cape over the shoulders of the sleeping king.

✦ ✦ ✦

After an hour or so, the young king began to stir. His eyes fluttered open, and he blinked in the flood of sunlight streaming in through the window. The knight walked over to the king's side. "You're awake." The king's white-blond hair was ruffled, and he frowned as he rubbed the sleep from his eyes. "How long was I asleep?" "An hour." "An hour?" the king echoed, sighing as he pulled his stack of documents toward him. "Why didn't you wake me earlier?" "My apologies, Your Majesty." The young knight bowed his head, and his dark hair fell over his eyes. "You looked like you needed the rest. I didn't want to wake you." "Never mind." The king smiled softly as he scanned the contents of the document in front of him. "It's a beautiful day. Why don't you go spend some time outside in the sun, Lier? I'd hate to keep you cooped up in here with me." Lier shook his head. "I don't mind. What about you, though? I think it would be good for you to get some fresh air." The young king set down his quill as he turned to look over at Lier. "It's true that I've been working all morning. Would you mind if I trouble you?" "Not at all." Lier found himself smiling faintly as he stepped over to the king's wheelchair. He wheeled the young king out of the study and out into the stone halls of the castle beyond.

Lier and August grew up together — Lier the son of a general, appointed as August's study companion and closest friend since childhood. But five years ago, August's brother, Prince Finnian, tried to push August off a castle balcony to secure the throne for himself. In the struggle, both princes fell; August survived, paralyzed from the waist down, while Finnian did not. Ever since, rumors painted August as his brother's murderer, and August has ruled as a young, guilt-ridden king — cared for always by Lier, his loyal knight and truest friend, who has never left his side.

One warm evening, a faerie assassin named Doe slips into August's study, testing Lier's skill before revealing she has no true intention of killing the king. She tells them of a grandmother in the fae forest who may be able to heal August's broken legs — for a price of trust neither Lier nor August is certain they can afford, given how many in the court want August dead. Despite August's fear of risking Lier's life among magic he cannot understand, Lier insists on investigating the fae city himself, and later persuades August to sneak out of the castle in disguise to be healed.

The elderly faerie heals August's legs and lessens the effects of the slow poison that has plagued him for years, while Doe secretly hands Lier evidence — a scrap of fabric — proving one of the court's ministers hired her to assassinate the king. Armed with this proof, August and Lier expose the conspiracy, banishing the traitors and clearing the way for August to finally rule freely and pursue the reforms he has long dreamed of.

Through it all, what began as loyalty between prince and knight deepens into something neither can deny: as August finally begins to walk again and the poison fades from his blood, he and Lier confess a love that has quietly grown since childhood — through pastries snuck between lessons, ointment applied to bruises, and years of quiet devotion neither ever thought could be spoken aloud. In that last month of summer, the sunlight streaming through the window has never been warmer, the land beyond brighter, nor the person at Lier's side more special.
