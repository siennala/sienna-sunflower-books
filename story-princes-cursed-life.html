The Prince's Cursed Life

By Sienna Sunflower

Legend has it that the first son of the emperor and the empress had a curse put on him from birth. Through whispered rumors, the story spread across the land that it was an evil witch who cursed the boy. The witch — once the emperor's lover — had grown bitterly jealous of the empress. After she cursed the prince, the witch vanished, never to be seen again.

Ming Junfeng had been working at the palace for nearly ten years, secretly trained since childhood by his father to become an assassin. His mission: infiltrate the inner palace and kill the "monster prince," whose curse forces him to consume a human heart every day — a curse that, should the prince die at the wrong hand, will instead be transferred onto the emperor himself. When Junfeng finally sneaks into the prince's chambers under cover of night, he discovers the prince, Yin Jin, was expecting him — and wanted to be killed all along, desperate for an end to a life he never chose.

✦ ✦ ✦

Junfeng can't bring himself to do it. Instead, an uneasy trust grows between them: Yin Jin conceals Junfeng's true identity from the empress, and the two begin secretly meeting, drawn together over books, chess, calligraphy, and quiet nights spent watching the stars from Yin Jin's isolated courtyard. Junfeng learns the truth of the curse from a captured witch and from a former acquaintance of the prince: the only way to break it is for Yin Jin to be betrayed and killed by the person he loves most. Torn between his mission and his growing feelings, Junfeng even orchestrates a series of arranged meetings between Yin Jin and eligible women, hoping one of them might become the one to end the curse in his place — but Yin Jin refuses to let anyone else get close enough to be hurt.

As weeks turn to months, Junfeng realizes there is no one else — he is the one Yin Jin loves most, and he has fallen for the prince in return. Unable to bear the weight of the curse and the innocent deaths it demands any longer, Junfeng makes an agonizing choice. One quiet night, as Yin Jin drifts toward sleep beside him, Junfeng plunges a hidden blade into his heart. Yin Jin doesn't fight back — he only asks, with his last breath, if Junfeng ever loved him at all. Junfeng lies and says no, unable to let the prince die burdened by grief for someone unworthy of him. Then, having ended the curse and everything it demanded of the innocent, Junfeng turns the blade on himself.

By morning, the empress finds them both, and the emperor — cured of the curse at last — is left to grieve a son and a devotion he never fully understood, as the kingdom's long era of terror finally comes to an end.
