Story Of The Maiden Jiselle

By Sienna Sunflower

Jiselle stood on the rocks by the ocean. The fog engulfed the land, and a harsh wind sent her hair and skirts whipping around her. The waves crashed loudly. She wrapped her arms around her body as she shivered from the cold. It didn't take long for the ship to emerge from the fog, drifting toward her. As it neared, Jiselle frantically hurried toward the docks. Once the ship docked, Jiselle stood there, scanning the faces of the young men disembarking. She took a step back, her heart sinking as the last of the men got off the ship.

Her younger brother, Rorus, never returned from the sea — one of many young sailors lured to their deaths by the enchanting songs of sirens. Disguising herself as a sailor, Jiselle sets out alone in a rowboat toward the fog-shrouded island where the sirens dwell, determined to find him. She discovers Rorus alive but enchanted, claimed as a lover by a siren named Mena, who is carrying his child. Jiselle bargains her own freedom for her brother's, staying behind as Mena's servant while Rorus returns safely home.

✦ ✦ ✦

Over the following months, Jiselle and Mena form an unlikely bond. When Jiselle learns that siren sons — unlike daughters, who inherit wings — are drowned at birth by ancient custom, she and Mena secretly build a hidden escape route through the wreckage-strewn waters to smuggle male infants to safety. When Mena's own son, Enro, is born, the two women flee the island together, raising him in a secluded home built near the graves of Jiselle's parents — a found family stitched together from grief, magic, and love.

Years later, when Enro is finally sent to live with his father, Rorus, tragedy strikes: Rorus is brutally attacked by his jealous wife, Ellalora, who kidnaps Enro after discovering the truth about his mother. In a desperate confrontation in the forest, Mena sacrifices her own wings to save her son, and Jiselle fights to protect them both. To heal Mena, Jiselle makes one final bargain with the sirens — trading away her own memories of everything that came before.

Years later, married to a man who found her floating, memoryless, at sea, Jiselle hears a haunting, familiar song on the wind — and everything comes rushing back. She runs toward the shore, toward the winged woman and the boy waiting for her there, remembering at last who she truly is.

✦ End ✦
