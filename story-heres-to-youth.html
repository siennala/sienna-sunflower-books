Here's To Youth

By Sienna Sunflower

Ouyang Hao placed the last dish on the rack. The sun had just set, and the last rays of the dying light filtered in through the grimy kitchen window. He absently fiddled with the strings of his apron before making his way over to the door. Pulling the trash bags stacked there along with him, Ouyang Hao pushed open the door. The darkening sky cast the alley in shadows, and Ouyang Hao found himself instinctively quickening his pace.

After throwing away the trash, Ouyang Hao started back into the restaurant. But just before he pulled open the door, his gaze landed on a figure lying at the end of the alley — a young man named Jiang Yiran, beaten and injured after being ambushed by thugs. What starts as a reluctant bowl of noodles traded for information turns into an unlikely partnership: both Ouyang Hao and Jiang Yiran have their own reasons to despise Sun Ning, a wealthy, manipulative classmate who blackmails Ouyang Hao's best friend Lili with a secretly recorded video, and who ruined Jiang Yiran's reputation and career prospects years earlier over a violent misunderstanding.

✦ ✦ ✦

Together, Ouyang Hao and Jiang Yiran devise a plan to protect Lili by flooding the internet with convincing fake videos of her, defusing the power of Sun Ning's blackmail before he can use it. As they work side by side — over shared bowls of noodles, late nights coding in an empty apartment, and a hanfu festival where they track down a witness to an old, unresolved injustice — their partnership deepens into an easy companionship neither expected. Along the way, both young men open up about the dreams they've kept hidden from their families: Ouyang Hao wants to open his own restaurant instead of pursuing the life science career his mother envisions for him, and Jiang Yiran, despite his talent with code, dreams simply of driving a taxi and being free of an office.

When Jiang Yiran finally gathers enough evidence to expose Sun Ning publicly, Sun Ning retaliates violently, leaving Jiang Yiran badly injured. Ouyang Hao rushes him to the hospital, and in the aftermath, cuts ties with his estranged father and embraces the life he actually wants. Years later, in the story's epilogue, Ouyang Hao owns his own restaurant, and Jiang Yiran — hair streaked with the gray of a decade gone by — drives a taxi by day and comes home to him every night. Two young men who once only shared bowls of noodles in a back alley now share a life, still toasting, as they always have, to their youth.

✦ End ✦
